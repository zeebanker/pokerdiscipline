KP's Bluff Discipline
Framework.

A complete decision system for low-to-mid stakes live MTTs. Builds the hand correctly, filters the bluff in the moment, enforces it through redundant layers.

Operating Default
"No river bluff jams unless proven otherwise."
You are opting out of bluffing, not opting in. Bluffs are earned, never assumed.

Thesis
02 / 18

The leak has two causes, and both must be addressed.

Most frameworks fix only the river decision. That's the symptom. The hand shape that produces the punt is set on the flop.

Dual causation
1

Upstream: hand-shape discipline

  • Under-bet draws when equity is max
  • Arrive at river with no plan
  • Treat past equity as current entitlement
2

Downstream: in-moment discipline

  • Bluff into capped-at-pair ranges
  • Rep hands you don't have in range
  • Fire against players who don't fold
Framework structure Part I addresses hand shape. Part II addresses in-moment filters. Part III adds redundancy.
Part I · Hand-Shape Discipline
03 / 18

Win the hand on the flop and turn, so the river is never a punt spot.

Most painful river decisions are the result of incorrect flop and turn play. Fix the cause and the symptom disappears.

Scope of Part I
  • Tag the table before you play a pot
  • Press equity when you have it
  • Bank equity; never treat it as owed
  • Pre-commit the river plan on the flop
Rule 00 · Tag the Table
04 / 18

Before any meaningful pot, you already know who folds and who doesn't.

Villain classification is a pre-session habit, not a pre-bluff step. Default assumption if untagged: no-fold.

Three player types
TypeProfileBluff Policy
A · CuriousCalls down, "let me see it"Never
B · StraightforwardStrong = bet, weak = foldClean spots
C · Thinking RegCapable of foldingSelective
Rule Zero Any villain who just doubled up with top pair is Type A for the next hour. No exceptions.
Rule 01 · Press Equity When You Have It
05 / 18

Big draws are bet bigger, not smaller. Under-betting is how punt spots are born.

When you have 12+ outs
  • Bet 70–80% pot on wet boards
  • You are often favorite or co-favorite
  • Build pot while equity is real
  • Charge weaker draws, set up turn barrel
What small probes do instead
  • Get called by everything
  • Create no fold equity
  • Build a pot you can't win without hitting
Key insight You don't earn the right to bluff the river by under-betting the flop. You forfeit it.
Rule 02 · Equity Is Banked, Not Owed
06 / 18

Flop equity is an opportunity on the flop. On the river, it is zero.

The trap
Feeling
"I had 15 outs. I deserve this pot."
Reality
9-high is 9-high. The outs were earlier.
The signal When you notice "but I had so much equity" or "I've put so much in" — that is the tell you are about to punt. Check-fold instead.
Rule 03 · Pre-Commit on the Flop
07 / 18

Decide your river plan on the flop, before emotion arrives.

The moment you arrive at the river is when ego, sunk cost, and frustration peak. Make the decision earlier, when you're calm.

On every flop you continue, decide
  • If I hit, what's my sizing on each street?
  • If I miss, am I barreling or giving up?
  • What turn cards change the plan?
Why this works Pre-commitment removes the in-moment decision. The plan exists. You execute, not deliberate.
Part II · In-Moment Checks
08 / 18

When Part I fails and you still feel the urge to bluff, these five checks catch you.

Run them in order at the table. If any one says "no," the bluff is off. Takes about 10 seconds.

The five checks
  • What has villain already done this hand? Certain earlier actions mean "I have a pair and I'm not folding."
  • Can villain actually have a weak hand here? Do I hold cards that block their strong hands? Can I name real hands they'd fold?
  • The gut check: would a typical live tournament player fold top pair in this spot?
  • Does my story make sense? If I actually had the hand I'm repping, would I have played it this way — and is it even in my range?
  • What's the move instead? Check-fold, or a small bet, beats a stack-off bluff every time.
Filter 01 · Auto-Blockers
09 / 18

If any signal from earlier streets occurred, do not jam the river.

Each independently indicates villain is committed to a one-pair hand they will not release.

Disqualifying signals
  • Villain check-called flop AND turn
  • Villain led turn
  • Villain raised turn
  • Villain is a Type A "Curious Caller"
  • Villain doubled up recently with a weak made hand
Translation They have a pair and are not folding it. Representation is worth zero against a hand, not a range.
Filter 02 · The Three Hard Criteria
10 / 18

Bluffs are justified only when all three are satisfied simultaneously.

Required conditions
I

Villain's range is capped

  • No strong hands likely
  • Mostly one-pair or weaker
  • River card changed their range, not just yours
II

You hold relevant blockers

  • Block strong calling hands
  • Unblock folding hands
III

You can name ≥5 realistic folds

  • Weak top pair, second pair
  • Missed draws that gave up
  • "I just don't believe you" is not a fold
If any condition fails Do not bluff.
Filter 03 · The Gut Check
11 / 18

One question resolves the majority of marginal bluff spots.

Decision framework
Ask yourself
"Would a random $1K WSOP player fold top pair here?"
If No Check
If Yes Proceed to Filters 04–05

Emotionally neutral. Pool-calibrated. Faster than the three criteria when the clock is ticking.

Filter 04 · Rep Hands in Your Range
12 / 18

You can only rep hands your line tells and your range contains.

Most bluffs fail not because villain decoded them, but because the story was imaginary to begin with.

Two diagnostic questions
  • "If I actually had that hand, would I bet this size on this street?"
  • "Is that hand even in my perceived range from this position?"
Common self-deception
Wished
"I'm repping KJ / AJ / overpair."
Actual
Your UTG range doesn't contain those hands, and overpairs bet bigger.
And remember Against Type A villains, representation is worth zero anyway. You are bluffing a hand, not a range.
Filter 05 · The Replacement Move
13 / 18

When the filters fail, the default is not "shove anyway" — it's one of two moves.

Default replacements
  • Check and give up. Losing the pot you were losing anyway is free.
  • Small blocker bet (25–33% pot). Folds out air, gets called by the same hands that call a shove — at 2–3x less cost.
Reframe You are not missing spots. You are removing losing plays. Saving a stack is earning a stack.
Part III · Backup Rules
14 / 18

When Parts I and II fail — and some night they will — these three backup rules catch you.

Tilt, momentum, "this one just feels right" — any single rule can break under pressure. Stacked rules do not all break at once.

Three backup rules
  • Flip the question. Don't ask "could this work?" — ask "can I prove this WILL work?"
  • Name what's at stake. Am I putting my tournament life at risk to fold out one pair?
  • Remember the last time. Every bluff that got snapped off was obvious to them, not you.
Backup 01 · Flip the Question
15 / 18

Stop asking "could this work?" Start asking "can I prove this will work?"

"Could" is a low bar. Almost any bluff "could" work. "Will" forces you to show your evidence.

Shift the default
Old question
"This could work."
New question
"Prove to me this WILL work."
To proceed, you must show
  • Villain is capable of folding in this spot
  • Your betting line looks like a real value hand to them
  • You can name specific hands they'd fold
If you can't prove all three No bluff. Not being sure isn't the same as having a green light.
Backup 02 · Name What's at Stake
16 / 18

Ask: "Am I risking my tournament life to fold out one pair?"

If yes → no bluff. Reframes the shove from "heroic" to "absurd."

Live players will call off with
  • Top pair
  • Overpairs
  • "I don't believe you"
  • Pair + emotional commitment from earlier streets
The reality You are risking everything to fold out hands that don't fold.
Backup 03 · Remember the Last Time
17 / 18

Every time you got snapped off bluffing, it looked obvious to them.

Because
  • They had a pair
  • They never considered folding
  • They were not reading your story — they were reading their cards
Burn this in The hero shove feels complex to you. It is a call decision to them. That asymmetry is the leak.
Combined Stack · Final Calibration
18 / 18

When you feel the urge to jam, run the stack.

The simple stack — any "yes" cancels the bluff
Did he put money in twice?
Yes → No Bluff
Would he fold top pair?
No → No Bluff
Is the river card a brick to his range?
Yes → No Bluff
Am I risking stack to fold one pair?
Yes → No Bluff
Am I feeling "owed" for prior equity?
Yes → No Bluff
The one line to remember
"Tag the table, press equity when you have it, bank it when you don't, bluff only when you can prove it — and pre-commit the plan on the flop."
Winning adjustment Bluff rarely. Value-bet relentlessly. Fold often. Protect the stack that makes the rest possible.
