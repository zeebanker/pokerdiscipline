S · Stack Reality
04 / 20

"Who covers who? What happens if I'm wrong?"

Key logic
  • Covered = elimination risk → tighten stacking thresholds
  • Ask: Am I playing for chips or for my tournament life?
  • Am I risking my tournament life to fold out one pair?
Reality Live players call off with top pair, overpairs, and "I don't believe you." You are risking everything to fold out hands that don't fold.
A · Action Credibility
05 / 20

"Does villain's line represent real strength?"

Credible strength lines in live play
  • Donk + call-off, check-raise jam, river raise = polarized, usually value-heavy live
  • Older/tight players → heavily weighted to value
Examples — all three were credible strength lines
  • A99 donk → strong Ax/9x/traps
  • Turn lead + jam → nutted range
  • River raise (tight player) → almost never bluff

E · Ego Filter
07 / 20

"Am I trying to win the hand… or avoid being wrong?"

Red flags
  • "He can't have it again"
  • "I'm under-repped"
  • "I'll trap / I'll show him"
Reality check Your river punt is pure ego override after correctly suspecting a boat.
R · Reversible Decision + Hard Rules
08 / 20

"Is there a lower-variance line?" If yes → take it unless you have strong edge.

Lower-variance defaults
  • Call > raise
  • Control pot > inflate
  • Preserve stack > maximize thin value
Three non-negotiable hard rules
  • One-pair = no stack-off vs coverage — unless you beat value range clearly or villain is proven spewy
  • If you raise and get jammed, ask: what worse does this? If unclear or none → pause, don't snap
  • River raises at low stakes = value until proven otherwise. People under-bluff massively, especially tight/older profiles
Your one-line table mantra "Covered + strong line + no worse calls = don't stack off."
Section II

River Bluff Discipline

River-specific filters. Use these when deciding whether to bluff jam — not whether to call one.

Auto-Blockers
10 / 20

If they put money in on two streets, you don't bluff them off it.

Each signal independently indicates villain has committed to a hand they will not release.

Instant NO BLUFF if any occurred
  • Villain check-called flop AND turn
  • Villain led turn
  • Villain raised turn
  • Villain profiles as a "curious caller"
Key Takeaway They have a pair, are emotionally committed, and call "to see it."
The Hard Rule
11 / 20

Bluffing is only justified when all three criteria are satisfied.

Required conditions
I

Villain's range is capped

  • No strong hands likely
  • Mostly one-pair / weak holdings
II

You hold relevant blockers

  • Block strong hands
  • Unblock folds
III

You can name ≥5 realistic folds

  • Weak top pair
  • Second pair
  • Missed draws that gave up
If any condition fails Do not bluff. Prove it WILL work — not that it could.
Board, Story & Missed Draws
12 / 20

Both board texture and betting line must support the story — with credible missed draws.

Dual requirement
I

Board is scary for them

  • Not just "looks scary" to you
II

Your line makes sense

  • Passive line → sudden jam = not believed
Missed draws check — all three must be true
  • Clear missed draws exist on the board
  • Villain's range can contain those draws
  • You can represent them logically given your line
The test Is this a complicated bluff against a simple player? If yes — don't do it. You're thinking ranges and blockers. They're thinking "I have a pair."

The 5-Second River Script
15 / 20

Execute this four-question sequence before every river jam.

Decision sequence
Did he show strength earlier?
Yes → No Bluff
Would he fold top pair?
No → No Bluff
Can I name 5 folds?
No → No Bluff
Does my line make sense?
No → No Bluff
If all 4 pass You may bluff. Expect this to be rare — that is correct.
River Jam Ban & Turn Discipline
16 / 20

Temporary training tool: ban all river bluff jams unless checked to twice.

Allowed
  • Check → check → jam
  • Clear weakness
Not allowed
  • Bet/call → jam
  • Call/call → jam
  • Any strength shown
Turn discipline rule
  • If you call turn with a draw, you are NOT obligated to bluff river
  • You are allowed to miss → check/fold
  • Right now you're "invested" → feel forced to bluff. Break that link.
