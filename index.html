The Discipline
Framework.

A decision system for eliminating unprofitable river bluffs in low-to-mid stakes live tournaments.

Step 00 · The Operating Default
"No river bluff jams unless proven otherwise."
You are opting out of bluffing, not opting in.
12 Pages · Read in 5 Min Confidential
Step 01 · Auto-Blockers
02 / 12

If any of four signals occurred earlier in the hand, do not jam the river.

Each signal independently indicates villain has committed to a one-pair hand they will not release.

Disqualifying signals
  • Villain check-called flop AND turn
  • Villain led turn
  • Villain raised turn
  • Villain profiles as a "curious caller"
Key Takeaway They have a pair and are not folding it.
Step 02 · The Hard Rule
03 / 12

Bluffing is only justified when all three criteria are satisfied simultaneously.

Required conditions
I

Villain's range is capped

  • No strong hands likely
  • Mostly one-pair / weak holdings
II

You hold relevant blockers

  • Block strong hands
  • Unblock folds
III

You can name ≥5 realistic folds

  • Weak top pair
  • Second pair
  • Missed draws that gave up
If any condition fails Do not bluff.
Step 03 · Practical Shortcut
04 / 12

A single diagnostic question resolves the majority of marginal bluff spots.

Decision framework
Ask yourself
"Would a random $1K WSOP player fold top pair here?"
If No Check
If Yes Proceed

This single question eliminates a disproportionate share of negative-EV jams.

Step 04 · Board + Story
05 / 12

Both board texture and betting line must support the story.

Dual requirement
I

Board is scary for them

  • Not just "looks scary" to you
II

Your line makes sense

  • Passive line → sudden jam = not believed
Population reality Live players rarely believe inconsistent stories.
Step 05 · Missed Draws
06 / 12

Bluffing requires credible, representable missed draws.

All three conditions must hold. A single failure invalidates the bluff.

Conditions required
  • Clear missed draws exist on the board
  • Villain's range can contain those draws
  • You can represent them logically given your line
If not You are blasting into pairs.

The 5-Second Script
08 / 12

Execute this four-question sequence before every river jam.

Decision sequence
Did he show strength earlier?
Yes → No Bluff
Would he fold top pair?
No → No Bluff
Can I name 5 folds?
No → No Bluff
Does my line make sense?
No → No Bluff
If all 4 pass You may bluff. Expect this to be rare — that is correct.
Default Line Reset
09 / 12

Replace the reflex default. Checking is the new baseline.

Before vs. after
Current State
Miss draw → jam
Target State
Miss draw → check and give up
Reframe You are not missing spots. You are removing losing plays.
Permitted Jam Conditions
10 / 12

Legitimate bluff jams exist, but satisfy all four criteria.

Green-light requirements
  • Villain checked multiple streets
  • Board changed in a way that hits your range
  • You can name the folds
  • Villain is capable of folding
Proceed These are the only real bluff jam spots in your games.
