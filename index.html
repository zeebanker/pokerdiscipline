Step 01 · Auto-Blockers
02 / 23

If any of four signals occurred earlier in the hand, do not jam the river.

Each signal independently indicates villain has committed to a one-pair hand they will not release.

Disqualifying signals
  • Villain check-called flop AND turn
  • Villain led turn
  • Villain raised turn
  • Villain profiles as a "curious caller"
Key Takeaway They have a pair and are not folding it.
Step 02 · The Hard Rule
03 / 23

Bluffing is only justified when all three criteria are satisfied simultaneously.

Required conditions
I

Villain's range is capped

  • No strong hands likely
  • Mostly one-pair / weak holdings
II

You hold relevant blockers

  • Block strong hands
  • Unblock folds
III

You can name ≥5 realistic folds

  • Weak top pair
  • Second pair
  • Missed draws that gave up
If any condition fails Do not bluff.
Step 03 · Practical Shortcut
04 / 23

A single diagnostic question resolves the majority of marginal bluff spots.

Decision framework
Ask yourself
"Would a random $1K WSOP player fold top pair here?"
If No Check
If Yes Proceed

This single question eliminates a disproportionate share of negative-EV jams.

Step 04 · Board + Story
05 / 23

Both board texture and betting line must support the story.

Dual requirement
I

Board is scary for them

  • Not just "looks scary" to you
II

Your line makes sense

  • Passive line → sudden jam = not believed
Population reality Live players rarely believe inconsistent stories.
Step 05 · Missed Draws
06 / 23

Bluffing requires credible, representable missed draws.

All three conditions must hold. A single failure invalidates the bluff.

Conditions required
  • Clear missed draws exist on the board
  • Villain's range can contain those draws
  • You can represent them logically given your line
If not You are blasting into pairs.

Rule 01 · Two-Street Commitment
13 / 23

If they put money in on two streets, you don't bluff them off it.

Applies when
  • Call flop + call turn
  • Bet turn + call
  • Any "sticky" line
Your action
  • No river bluff jams. Ever.
Why it works These players have a pair, are emotionally committed, and call "to see it."
Rule 02 · Stack Risk Filter
14 / 23

Before jamming, ask: "Am I risking my tournament life to fold out one pair?"

If yes → NO BLUFF.

Translation · Live players will call off with
  • Top pair
  • Overpairs
  • "I don't believe you"
The reality You are risking everything to fold out hands that don't fold.
Rule 03 · Reverse Burden of Proof
15 / 23

Flip your logic. Prove the bluff will work — not that it could.

Logic reset
Old (current)
"This could work."
New
"Prove to me this WILL work."
You must prove
  • They can fold
  • You rep value
  • You have folds
If you can't prove it No bluff.
Rule 04 · Call Type Classification
16 / 23

Classify villain before bluffing.

Three player types
A

"Curious Caller" — most common

  • Calls down
  • Doesn't like folding
  • "Let me see it"
B

"Straightforward"

  • Bets when strong
  • Gives up when weak
C

"Thinking Reg"

  • Capable of folding
  • Understands ranges
Action by type
TypeProfileBluff Policy
A · CuriousCalls downNever big
B · StraightforwardStrong = betClean spots only
C · Thinking RegCan foldSelective
Your mistake You're treating Type A like Type C.
Rule 05 · River Jam Ban
17 / 23

For your next sessions: ban all river bluff jams unless checked to twice.

Allowed
  • Check → check → jam
  • Clear weakness
Not allowed
  • Bet/call → jam
  • Call/call → jam
  • Any strength shown
Why this works Removes your biggest leak instantly. Forces you to earn bluff spots. Retrains instincts.

If the answer includes

  • Sets
And those hands are common Your bluff is bad.

Rule 08 · Turn Discipline Rule
20 / 23

Most bad river jams start with bad turn decisions.

The rule
  • If you call turn with a draw, you are NOT obligated to bluff river
  • You are allowed to miss → check/fold
This is huge for you Right now you're "invested" → feel forced to bluff. Break that link.
